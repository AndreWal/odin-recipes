Lasagna Recipe

Description

Making lasagna starts with a good foundation: the sauce and the filling. Most people use a rich tomato-based meat sauce, made by browning minced beef (or a beef–pork mix) with onions and garlic, then simmering it with tomatoes, herbs like oregano and basil, and a bit of salt and pepper. While that bubbles away, you can prepare a simple béchamel (white sauce) by melting butter, stirring in flour, and slowly whisking in milk until it thickens, seasoning with salt, pepper, and maybe a pinch of nutmeg. Grate plenty of cheese—usually mozzarella for meltiness and Parmesan for flavor. You can also swap the meat for vegetables like spinach, zucchini, or mushrooms if you want a veggie version.

Assembling the lasagna is a bit like building a tasty brick wall. You start with a thin layer of sauce in the baking dish, then add a layer of lasagna sheets (pre-cooked or no-boil, depending on what you’re using). On top of that go spoonfuls of béchamel, some of the meat or vegetable sauce, and a generous sprinkle of cheese. Then you repeat: pasta, sauces, cheese—until you reach the top. Finish with lots of cheese so it gets a golden crust. Bake it until the pasta is tender and the top is bubbly and slightly browned. The hardest part is waiting: let it rest for 10–15 minutes before cutting, so the layers set and you get neat slices instead of a delicious but messy avalanche.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, cooking until softened.
  3. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  4. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes. Gradually whisk in the milk and cook until thickened. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer with noodles, béchamel sauce, meat sauce, and a sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of cheese on top.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  8. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Enjoy!
